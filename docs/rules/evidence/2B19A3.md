## abilityRules

1. Dreamer chooses a player other than themself or a Traveller and learns exactly one GOOD character and one EVIL character.
2. Normal Dreamer information asserts the proposition: the target's true character equals exactly one of the two reported characters.
3. GOOD means a Townsfolk or Outsider character; EVIL means a Minion or Demon character. This classification is the character's native category, not the player's current alignment.
4. Under an effective Vortox, Townsfolk ability information must be false. For Dreamer, the proposition in item 2 must be false: neither reported role equals the target's settlement-time current true role.
5. Forced-false Dreamer information still has the Dreamer output shape: exactly one GOOD role and exactly one EVIL role.
6. The target truth is the character in settlement-time `CurrentCharacterState`; assignment-time or later state cannot replace the truth at resolution.
7. A delivery is a historical fact. Later character, alignment, impairment, or Demon changes do not recompute or rewrite it.

## interactions

### Effective Vortox applicability

The forced-false path requires canonical settlement-time proof of one current Vortox identity matching the official catalog, one continuous active Vortox tenure, and no applicable Vortox `DRUNK` or `POISONED` impairment. Missing, duplicate, conflicting, stale, cross-linked, or malformed proof is not permission to assume Vortox effectiveness.

### Dreamer source and target

The path is base Dreamer only. Canonical task, V3 opportunity, base Dreamer ability instance, active Dreamer tenure, source player/seat/role, target V2, roster, catalog, and settlement revision must cross-bind. Target legality remains the accepted 2B19A2 boundary.

### False pair

For target true role `T`, legal Vortox output must satisfy:

- `goodRole.characterType` is Townsfolk or Outsider;
- `evilRole.characterType` is Minion or Demon;
- `goodRole.roleId != T.roleId`;
- `evilRole.roleId != T.roleId`;
- GOOD and EVIL roles are distinct exact catalog entries.

The false role may be in play. External sources do not require out-of-play candidates.

### Outcome ledger and Mathematician

Official and Chinese Mathematician sources say to learn how many players' abilities worked abnormally due to another character's ability. The counted unit is a qualifying player, not the number of incidents, event records, ability facts, or deviations. A player qualifies only when that player's ability actually worked abnormally and the cause was another character's ability. A normal result does not qualify. Vortox-caused false Townsfolk information is an explicit official example of a qualifying abnormal result. Therefore a successfully forced-false Dreamer delivery, including one delivered while the source Dreamer is represented drunk or poisoned under an effective Vortox, qualifies the Dreamer player because the abnormal result is caused by Vortox.

The product represents that official conclusion as:

- `outcomeStatus = ABNORMAL`;
- `causeKind = VORTOX_FALSE_INFORMATION`;
- `causedByAnotherCharacterAbility = true`.

The closed evidence set must bind source event, task, action opportunity, character state, target role at revision, Dreamer tenure, Vortox tenure/applicability evidence, and Dreamer delivery. This ledger encoding is a product contract, not an official evidence schema. Mathematician later counts the Dreamer source as one distinct abnormal player even if a pure-policy input contains multiple qualifying abnormal facts for that source.

The external sources do not define this simulator's event-sequence window, resolving-instance exclusion, numeric hand-signal domain, duplicate-holder ordering, or stable selection. Those remain separately authorized product policies:

- `BOTC-SIM-MATHEMATICIAN-FIRST-NIGHT-WINDOW-V1` fixes the first-night event-sequence bounds and creates no general dawn/reset rule;
- `BOTC-SIM-MATHEMATICIAN-OWN-ABILITY-EXCLUSION-V1` excludes only the resolving source's current Mathematician ability instance and current resolution;
- `BOTC-SIM-MATHEMATICIAN-NUMERIC-DOMAIN-V1` fixes the current 12-player simulator domain at `0..11` and is not exercised by this ledger-only regression;
- `BOTC-SIM-MATHEMATICIAN-DUPLICATE-HOLDER-TEMPORAL-V1` defines accepted duplicate-holder scheduling and observation boundaries without claiming an official multi-holder ruling.

Slice 2B19A3 changes no Mathematician production, does not settle or deliver Mathematician information, and uses these policies only to preserve the accepted downstream consumer contract.

## drunkennessRules

A drunk Dreamer normally has no functioning ability and may receive correct or incorrect apparent information. That non-Vortox information freedom is out of scope.

If Vortox is effective, a source Dreamer who is also represented `DRUNK` must still receive a false pair. Hidden canonical evidence retains the source impairment, while the controlling output and ledger cause remain Vortox-constrained false information. Source impairment must not leak through player or AI projection.

## poisoningRules

A poisoned Dreamer normally has no functioning ability and may receive correct or incorrect apparent information. That non-Vortox information freedom is out of scope.

If Vortox is effective, a source Dreamer who is also represented `POISONED` must still receive a false pair. The impairment remains hidden canonical evidence; the terminal abnormal cause is `VORTOX_FALSE_INFORMATION`, not merely `SOURCE_POISONING`.

No Dashii adjacency/poisoning derivation remains out of scope and must not be inferred from an absent marker.

## VortoxRules

1. While Vortox is alive and effective, Townsfolk abilities yield false information.
2. Dreamer is a Townsfolk role, so both reported roles must differ from the target's true character for the Dreamer proposition to be false.
3. An effective Vortox overrides the ordinary permission to give a drunk or poisoned Dreamer true information; the pair must remain false.
4. A drunk or poisoned Vortox has no functioning Vortox ability. With canonically represented Vortox impairment, an otherwise effective source returns to the accepted normal V2 path; if the Dreamer source itself is impaired and Vortox is ineffective, the existing non-Vortox source-impaired fail-closed boundary remains.
5. The repository has no general life/death behavior for this path. This evidence does not claim that a dead Vortox can be evaluated or that dead-Vortox behavior is implemented.
6. Missing or conflicting Vortox identity, tenure, revision, or impairment applicability must fail closed rather than silently choosing normal or forced-false information.

## characterChangeRules

- Target truth is the settlement-time current character. A target change before settlement changes which role must be excluded from both reported fields.
- A target change after delivery does not rewrite stored knowledge or ledger facts.
- Dreamer and Vortox source applicability use current canonical character state plus continuous tenure provenance at settlement.
- General character-change production, registration, Pit-Hag flows, gained Dreamer, and other-night recomputation are outside scope.

## alignmentChangeRules

Character and player alignment remain separate. An alignment-only change does not change:

- the target's true character;
- whether a reported character belongs in the GOOD or EVIL field;
- whether either reported role equals the target true role;
- the Dreamer/Vortox character ability identity.

General alignment-change production is outside scope.

## storytellerDiscretion

Official rules allow the Storyteller to choose any legal GOOD/EVIL false pair satisfying the Dreamer and Vortox constraints. The product may choose the stable smallest legal GOOD and EVIL role IDs using code-unit order to make simulation deterministic. That is a product policy subset, not an official unique choice, and must not depend on locale, catalog insertion order, randomness, or player-visible truth markers.

Free Storyteller selection, candidate-set persistence, and final-choice recording remain explicitly unsupported.
