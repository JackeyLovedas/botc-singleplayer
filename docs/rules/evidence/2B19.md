## officialDreamerRule

Each night, the Dreamer chooses a player other than themself or a Traveller and learns exactly one good character and one evil character, exactly one of which is the chosen player’s true character.

“Good character” means Townsfolk or Outsider. “Evil character” means Minion or Demon. It describes the character’s native type, not the player’s current alignment.

For a Townsfolk or Outsider target, normal information contains that true good character plus any Minion or Demon character. For a Minion or Demon target, normal information contains that true evil character plus any Townsfolk or Outsider character.

## chineseDreamerRule

The Chinese Wiki independently states the same contract:

- choose a player other than the Dreamer or a Traveller;
- learn one good character and one evil character;
- the target is one of those two characters;
- for a Townsfolk/Outsider target, the false character may be any Minion/Demon;
- for a Minion/Demon target, the false character may be any Townsfolk/Outsider.

Its Vortox example selects an actual Vortox but reports Oracle and No Dashii, confirming that a false Dreamer pair contains neither the target’s true character nor a change to the one-good/one-evil output shape.

## vortoxFalseInformationRule

While an effective Vortox is alive, information produced by Townsfolk abilities must be false. This remains mandatory when the receiving Townsfolk player is drunk or poisoned.

For Dreamer’s proposition “the target is one of these two characters,” false means the target is neither reported character. Therefore:

- `selectedGoodRole != targetTrueRole`;
- `selectedEvilRole != targetTrueRole`;
- exactly one GOOD character and one EVIL character are still reported.

The Vortox does not wake to kill on the first night, but its continuous Townsfolk-false-information ability applies on the first night while it is alive and effective.

A dead, drunk, or poisoned Vortox has no effective ability under the general States and Abilities rules. If the simulator cannot prove Vortox applicability at the Dreamer resolution boundary, it must not silently assume either truth or falsehood.

## philosopherGainedTiming

The Philosopher gains the chosen good character’s ability without becoming that character. If the gained ability acts at night, the Philosopher acts when that character normally acts. If it is a first-night ability, it acts that same night.

The fixed official nightsheet revision records:

- first night: Philosopher at zero-based index `13`, Dreamer at index `60`;
- other nights: Philosopher at index `10`, Vortox at index `46`, Dreamer at index `78`.

For this slice, only first-night Dreamer is in scope.

The approved `BOTC-SIM-PHILOSOPHER-FIRST-NIGHT-SCHEDULING-V1` simulator tie-break applies at the Dreamer catalog position:

1. base Dreamer;
2. Philosopher-gained Dreamer tasks by ascending source seat;
3. equal-seat tasks by stable task-ID code-unit order.

This override supplies only deterministic same-position ordering and does not alter Dreamer, Vortox, or Philosopher ability semantics.

## legalTargetRule

A Dreamer target must:

- exist at the resolution boundary;
- not be the resolving Dreamer source player;
- not be a Traveller.

Travellers are not currently modeled. Their absence does not authorize marking Traveller targeting complete.

## goodCandidateRule

The GOOD candidate domain consists of known Townsfolk and Outsider roles in the historical role-catalog snapshot.

- Normal GOOD target: the selected GOOD role must be the target’s true current role.
- Normal EVIL target: any legal GOOD role may be the false role.
- Vortox: any legal GOOD role may be selected except the target’s true role.
- Source-impaired without Vortox: the target’s true GOOD role may be included or omitted because drunk/poisoned information may be true or false.

No external rule requires a false GOOD role to be out of play. In-play roles are legal. Storyteller discretion determines which legal role is shown.

## evilCandidateRule

The EVIL candidate domain consists of known Minion and Demon roles in the historical role-catalog snapshot.

- Normal EVIL target: the selected EVIL role must be the target’s true current role.
- Normal GOOD target: any legal EVIL role may be the false role.
- Vortox: any legal EVIL role may be selected except the target’s true role.
- Source-impaired without Vortox: the target’s true EVIL role may be included or omitted because drunk/poisoned information may be true or false.

No external rule requires a false EVIL role to be out of play. The official guidance positively contemplates showing an in-play Minion, and the official Evil Twin/Artist example uses an in-play good alternative.

## targetTrueRoleRule

The relevant truth is the target’s true character at the time the Dreamer information resolves.

The repository’s `settlement-time CurrentCharacterState` is the product representation of that rules moment. Initial assignment and later state cannot replace the target character that existed at resolution.

Character type, not the target player’s alignment, determines which side is the true role:

- Townsfolk/Outsider character → true GOOD role;
- Minion/Demon character → true EVIL role.

Under normal information exactly one selected role equals `targetTrueRole`. Under effective Vortox neither selected role equals it.

## sourceDrunkRule

A drunk source player has no functioning ability, but the Storyteller continues the apparent procedure and may give correct or incorrect information. Incorrect information is allowed, not mandatory.

For the Dreamer-shaped interaction, the apparent output remains one GOOD and one EVIL character. Without Vortox, either of these outcomes is legal:

- the pair contains the target’s true character;
- the pair excludes the target’s true character.

A deterministic simulator may prefer a fully false legal pair, but that preference is a recorded simulation policy rather than an official rule claim.

## sourcePoisonRule

Poisoning has the same relevant effect as drunkenness:

- the source player has no functioning ability;
- the apparent Dreamer procedure continues;
- correct or incorrect information may be given;
- incorrect information is optional without Vortox;
- one GOOD and one EVIL character remain the apparent output.

The simulator must preserve canonical poisoning evidence separately from drunkenness even though their rule effect is equivalent.

## vortoxPlusImpairmentRule

Vortox takes precedence over the ordinary freedom to give a drunk or poisoned player true information.

If an effective Vortox is alive, a drunk or poisoned Philosopher/Dreamer source must still receive a false Dreamer pair:

- exactly one GOOD character;
- exactly one EVIL character;
- neither equals the target’s true character.

The hidden evidence may retain the source impairment, but the controlling result is Vortox-constrained false information. For outcome-ledger purposes, the abnormal cause is `VORTOX_FALSE_INFORMATION`, not merely source drunkenness or poisoning.

## currentRoleTiming

The target character and source effectiveness are evaluated when the Dreamer interaction resolves.

The rules do not define a repository field named `CurrentCharacterState`; mapping that instant to the settlement-time canonical state is a product representation consistent with the external timing rule.

A later character or alignment change does not retroactively modify what was shown earlier.

## characterChangeInteraction

- If the target changed character before resolution, Dreamer evaluates the new current character.
- If the target changes character after delivery, the delivered information remains historical.
- The Philosopher gains an ability but does not become the Dreamer; their true character remains Philosopher.
- A Philosopher-gained Dreamer resolution must therefore distinguish the source player’s character from the gained ability role.

General Pit-Hag changes and other-night character-change execution remain outside this slice except for consuming already represented settlement-time state.

## alignmentChangeRule

Character and alignment are independent. Dreamer learns characters, not player alignments.

Changing the target player’s alignment without changing their character does not change which character is the correct Dreamer answer or whether the role belongs in the GOOD or EVIL candidate field.

## registrationBoundary

General registration effects are not authorized in 2B19. Spy, Recluse, and comparable “might register as” interactions require separate rule evidence and implementation.

Within the fixed Sects & Violets catalog used by this slice, candidate truth is based on the represented actual current character. No registration behavior may be inferred or marked complete.

## storytellerDiscretion

The official rule gives the Storyteller discretion to choose any legal false character of the required type and offers strategic guidance. It does not require random choice, smallest-role choice, or an out-of-play role.

A deterministic simulator may choose a stable legal candidate from the historical catalog, including an in-play role, provided that:

- all candidates satisfy the external rule;
- the simulation policy and candidate snapshot are recorded;
- the result is never described as the official Storyteller’s unique choice;
- no random, clock, locale, input-order, or incidental-map ordering is treated as rule truth.

Free Storyteller candidate choice remains unsupported.

## baseV1Boundary

Accepted base V1 Dreamer history remains exactly replayable:

- existing V1 payload shape is unchanged;
- existing V1 three-event batch is unchanged;
- existing no-effective-Vortox base behavior remains compatible;
- historical deliveries are not recomputed.

V1 does not preserve enough Vortox/candidate evidence to authorize a new Vortox-constrained delivery. A new V1 command encountering an effective Vortox must fail closed without delivery, settlement, receipt consumption, or game-version change.

## baseV2Boundary

New V2 base Dreamer behavior may support:

- normal information;
- represented source drunkenness;
- represented source poisoning;
- effective Vortox;
- Vortox plus source impairment;
- settlement-time target character;
- canonical candidate snapshot;
- source-only historical projection;
- a terminal outcome-ledger fact.

The evidence does not authorize modifying the exact V1 payload.

## gainedV1Boundary

Accepted V1 plan/insertion history remains replayable, but gained V1 Dreamer settlement is not authorized.

V1 gained scheduling does not establish the accepted V2 normal-position timing and lacks the complete V2 source/candidate/Vortox evidence chain. It must not be silently migrated, reordered, or emitted as V2. New gained V1 execution must fail closed without consuming the command ID.

## gainedV2Boundary

The source of a Philosopher-gained Dreamer ability is the Philosopher player who gained that ability, not the original Dreamer.

Required rule identity:

- source player/seat: the Philosopher holder;
- source character: Philosopher;
- ability role: Dreamer;
- entitlement: the exact Philosopher choice/grant and resulting gained ability instance;
- timing: Dreamer’s first-night position under scheduling V2.

If a base Dreamer is in play, the Philosopher’s duplication makes that base Dreamer player drunk. That impairment affects the base Dreamer player only. It does not automatically impair the Philosopher-gained Dreamer source.

The gained source is impaired only when that Philosopher player has their own applicable canonical drunkenness or poisoning. If the Philosopher becomes drunk or poisoned, their gained ability is ineffective and the original duplicated Dreamer becomes sober under the Philosopher rule.

## candidateAvailability

A rules-valid resolution requires one legal GOOD role and one legal EVIL role from the historical catalog.

Additional requirements by path:

- normal GOOD target: target true role plus at least one legal EVIL candidate;
- normal EVIL target: target true role plus at least one legal GOOD candidate;
- effective Vortox: at least one non-target legal role on the target’s native side and at least one legal role on the opposite side;
- impaired without Vortox: at least one legal role on each side; if no fully false pair exists, a true-containing pair remains rule-legal.

Unknown roles, sparse arrays, duplicates, wrong native types, or absence of a required false candidate are not legal evidence. The simulator must fail closed rather than invent a role or use model memory. Candidate shortage is a retryable product dependency failure, not a new BOTC rule.
