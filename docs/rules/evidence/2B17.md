## Rules

### officialAbilitySummary

Clockmaker is a Townsfolk whose start-knowing ability gives, on its first night, the number of seated steps from a Demon to its nearest Minion. Counting starts with the player next to the Demon and ends with the Minion.

### chineseAbilitySummary

钟表匠在自己的首个夜晚得知恶魔与爪牙之间的最近距离，邻座为 `1`。中文版还 explicitly states:

- calculate immediately before waking from the current situation;
- enumerate every distinct Demon–Minion player pair;
- take the smallest distance;
- dead players remain eligible;
- return `0` in the exceptional absence of a Demon or Minion;
- Summoner registers as a Demon to Clockmaker.

### abilityRules

1. Identify Demon and Minion by character type or an applicable registration decision, never by alignment alone.
2. A native good Demon still counts as a Demon.
3. A native evil Townsfolk does not become a Minion.
4. Pair members must be different players.
5. Calculate both directions around the circle and take the shorter direction.
6. Enumerate all legal Demon–Minion pairs and take the minimum.
7. The delivered fact is private historical information fixed at delivery.

### firstNightOrder

Pinned nightsheet positions, one-based including `dusk`:

- Philosopher: `14`
- Minion info: `20`
- Demon info: `24`
- Snake Charmer: `37`
- Clockmaker: `60`
- Dreamer: `61`
- Seamstress: `62`

Thus a base Clockmaker resolves after Snake Charmer. The repository’s Philosopher-gained insertion is after `PHILOSOPHER_ACTION` and before `MINION_INFO`, consistent with immediately using a gained first-night-only ability that night.

### otherNightOrder

Clockmaker has no ordinary other-night entry. Official Philosopher rules explicitly give the example that a Philosopher gaining Clockmaker on night three receives Clockmaker information that night. This is an acquired-ability trigger, not recurring Clockmaker other-night behavior.

### distanceDefinition

For distinct Demon seat `d` and Minion seat `m` on a canonical 12-seat ring:

`distance(d,m) = min(clockwise(d,m), counterClockwise(d,m))`

This is the number of seat-to-seat steps and equals “players between them plus one.”

### clockwiseDistanceDefinition

`clockwise(d,m) = (m - d + 12) mod 12`

For distinct seats, its value is `1..11`.

### counterClockwiseDistanceDefinition

`counterClockwise(d,m) = (d - m + 12) mod 12`

For distinct seats, its value is `1..11`.

### nearestMinionRule

For one Demon reference and all Minion references, calculate every distinct pair and return the minimum pair distance. Adjacent seats therefore yield `1`.

### multipleDemonRule

The Chinese role detail requires every distinct Demon–Minion combination to be traversed. The official Glossary says each living Demon counts as “The Demon” in a multiple-Demon game. Registration may introduce further Demon references. The formal result is the minimum over every legal distinct pair. Current fixed S&V canonical history cannot produce multiple Demons, so this rule is sourced but unsupported by this slice.

### multipleMinionRule

Every current canonical Minion must be paired with the Demon references. A 12-player supported setup has two Minions, so multiple-Minion traversal is not optional: it is the normal reachable case.

### registrationRule

- A Spy may register as good and as a Townsfolk or Outsider, including while dead; this can make an actual Minion not register as a Minion for a detection.
- A Recluse may register as evil and as a Minion or Demon, including while dead.
- A Summoner specifically registers as the Demon to Clockmaker.
- Registration is a Storyteller decision per relevant detection and is not equivalent to changing actual character or alignment.
- The current fixed S&V catalog contains none of Spy, Recluse, or Summoner and has no registration engine. The safe bounded identity layer is therefore `NATIVE_CHARACTER_TYPE_ONLY`, explicitly not registration support.

### currentStateTimingRule

Use `CurrentCharacterState` at the Clockmaker information task’s own settlement revision, not initial assignment or task-plan creation state. Chinese Clockmaker explicitly says to calculate from the current situation immediately before waking. Official Abilities says new characters gain their abilities immediately and first-night-only abilities function that night.

### deadPlayerRule

Dead players remain included in Clockmaker pairing. Death does not remove character type, and the Chinese Clockmaker detail explicitly includes dead players. Current first-night canonical history has no modeled pre-Clockmaker death/life transition, so death interaction remains unsupported.

### travellerRule

Traveller is its own character type. An evil Traveller is not a Minion; the official and Chinese Clockmaker examples produce distance `2` despite an adjacent evil Traveller. Fixed 12-player S&V canonical history has no Travellers.

### noDemonRule

If there is no legal Demon reference after applicable registration, the sourced Clockmaker result is `0`. This history is unreachable in the current fixed S&V model and may be fail-closed rather than falsely claimed as supported.

### noMinionRule

If there is no legal Minion reference after applicable registration, the sourced Clockmaker result is `0`. This history is unreachable in the current fixed S&V model and may be fail-closed rather than falsely claimed as supported.

### zeroInformationRule

`0` is a formally valid Clockmaker output only for the exceptional absence of at least one required character type/reference set. It is not a native distance between two distinct seated players.

### sourceDrunkRule

A drunk player has no ability but is treated as though they do. For information, the Storyteller may give incorrect information but is not required to do so. Correct and false Clockmaker distances are therefore both legal candidates without Vortox. Correct information must not be removed from rule legality merely because a deterministic simulation normally chooses harm.

### sourcePoisonRule

Poisoning has the same ability-effect semantics as drunkenness. Correct and false Clockmaker distances are both legal candidates without Vortox. Current accepted event history cannot poison a player while that player currently holds Clockmaker; this rule is sourced but that canonical execution path is unsupported.

### vortoxRule

While an effective living Vortox exists, every Townsfolk ability’s information must be false. For Clockmaker, select only a candidate unequal to the rule-correct distance.

### vortoxPlusImpairmentRule

Vortox’s false-information requirement still applies when the Townsfolk source is drunk or poisoned. Source impairment does not permit the correct value under Vortox. This priority is explicit on the official Vortox page.

An impairment of the Vortox itself would disable its ability under general States rules, but current accepted S&V history cannot leave an impaired player currently holding Vortox. This is distinct from source impairment.

### philosopherInteraction

A Philosopher may gain Clockmaker and receives the information that night, even on a later night. If an original Clockmaker is in play, that original player becomes drunk. In the current first-night model:

- the gained `CLOCKMAKER_INFORMATION` task is inserted immediately after Philosopher and before Minion info;
- it uses the state revision at that inserted task’s settlement;
- the original Clockmaker’s base task remains later and resolves as a drunk source.

### snakeCharmerInteraction

Snake Charmer acts before base Clockmaker. If Snake Charmer selects the Demon:

- the former Snake Charmer becomes the current evil Demon;
- the former Demon becomes the good poisoned Snake Charmer;
- the base Clockmaker calculation must use that updated Demon seat and current character types.

A first-night Philosopher-gained Clockmaker task is inserted before Snake Charmer and therefore uses its earlier settlement state instead.

### characterChangeInteraction

Character changes before the particular Clockmaker settlement change which players have Demon or Minion types. Use current role snapshots. Do not recompute a delivered historical Clockmaker fact after a later character change.

### alignmentChangeInteraction

Character and alignment are independent. Alignment changes alone do not change Demon/Minion character type. Good Demons count; evil Townsfolk do not. Snake Charmer’s combined character/alignment swap is resolved from the new character snapshots, not by selecting current evil players.

### storytellerDiscretion

Storyteller discretion exists in:

- registration choices;
- choosing correct or incorrect information for drunk/poisoned sources;
- choosing among false candidates under Vortox.

It does not alter native circular geometry. A deterministic single-player policy may select one member of the rule-legal set, but that policy must be separately identified as simulation policy and must never be described as the complete official Storyteller discretion.

### legalInformationCandidateDomain

For the fixed 12-seat game:

- native distinct-pair truth distances: `{1,2,3,4,5,6}`;
- exceptional no-Demon/no-Minion output: `{0}`;
- complete legal numeric output domain: `{0,1,2,3,4,5,6}`.

Derivation:

1. Adjacent distinct seats are distance `1`.
2. On a 12-seat ring, the shorter of clockwise and counterclockwise paths is at most `6`.
3. No other positive integer can be a shortest 12-seat distance.
4. Chinese Clockmaker explicitly adds `0` for the absent-type cases.
5. False Clockmaker information must remain a Clockmaker distance output, so false candidates come from the same domain rather than arbitrary integers.

Candidate legality:

- effective source, no Vortox: `{ruleCorrectDistance}`;
- impaired source, no Vortox: all `{0..6}`, including the correct value;
- effective or impaired source with active Vortox: `{0..6} \ {ruleCorrectDistance}`.

A missing false candidate must fail closed, although this finite domain always contains alternatives to one true value.

## drunkennessRules

A drunk Clockmaker still receives an apparent Clockmaker number. Correct and incorrect candidates are both legal absent Vortox. Original Clockmaker drunkenness caused by a Philosopher choosing Clockmaker is canonically reachable.

## poisoningRules

A poisoned Clockmaker has the same information legality as a drunk Clockmaker. No accepted producer can currently create a canonical history where a player remains Clockmaker while poisoned.

## VortoxRules

An effective living Vortox forces false Townsfolk information, even for a drunk or poisoned Clockmaker. Candidate truth, source effectiveness, Vortox constraint, and deterministic selection reason must remain separate facts.

## characterChangeRules

Resolve native type sets from the settlement revision’s current role snapshots. Snake Charmer supplies the only currently reachable pre-base-Clockmaker role change. Later changes cannot rewrite delivered history.

## alignmentChangeRules

Never substitute `currentAlignment === EVIL` for `characterType === DEMON/MINION`. Alignment changes do not themselves alter Clockmaker identity classification.
