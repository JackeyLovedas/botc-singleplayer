## abilityRules

1. Dreamer chooses another player; the Dreamer cannot choose themself and cannot
   choose a Traveller.
2. A functioning Dreamer learns one good character and one evil character, one
   of which is the chosen player's actual character.
3. When the Philosopher gains Dreamer while a Dreamer is already in play, the
   already-in-play base Dreamer becomes drunk because of the Philosopher's
   ability.
4. The accepted 2B20A stream independently plans the original base Dreamer's
   `DREAMER_ACTION`. The Philosopher-gained Dreamer task cannot replace or
   settle that task.
5. The exact settlement uses the apparent Dreamer action: legal target, apparent
   one-good/one-evil delivery, and formal settlement of the real base task.
6. The base Dreamer is the delivery and outcome-fact source even though the
   Philosopher is the causal source of the impairment.
7. Private delivery is historical knowledge. Impairment provenance, actual
   truth, candidate sets, and Storyteller reasoning remain canonical secrets.

## drunkennessRules

1. A drunk character has no ability, but the Storyteller pretends the ability
   functions.
2. The drunk player is not told that they are drunk.
3. The Storyteller may give an information ability apparently valid information
   that is true or false within its structural constraints.
4. The apparent Dreamer delivery still contains exactly one good and one evil
   role.
5. A true pair contains the target's settlement-time actual character; a false
   pair excludes it.
6. False delivery is a separate legal Storyteller choice, not mechanical
   inversion of a true pair.
7. Projection must not reveal drunkenness, Philosopher provenance, truth
   classification, current Demon, candidates, policy metadata, or ledger facts.

## poisoningRules

- Poisoning and drunkenness both prevent an ability from working, but their
  provenance is distinct.
- 2B20A does not add a poisoned-Dreamer success path.
- `POISONED`, conflicting, ambiguous, or noncanonical impairment remains
  unsupported and fail closed.
- No Dashii derivation remains a future slice.
