- `[HIGH]` Every night, the Cerenovus chooses a player and a good character. During the following day and night, that player is expected to make a sincere effort to convince the group that they are the selected character; otherwise the Storyteller may execute them.

## legalTargetRules

- `[HIGH]` The operational wording is “any player.” The target need not be alive, good, a standard character, or different from the Cerenovus.
- The target choice and selected-character choice are independent: any legal player may be instructed to claim any legal selected character.
- A legal-looking choice still occurs while the source is drunk or poisoned; deterministic canonical replay should record that selection even though it creates no real ability effect.
- Current Slice 2B16 may validate only roster membership because life state and Travellers are not modeled. It must not describe that limited check as complete target legality.

## legalGoodCharacterRules

- `[HIGH]` Normally the selected character must be a Townsfolk or Outsider icon on the current character sheet/script.
- `[HIGH]` It may be in play or not in play. The official Glossary defines characters as potentially in or out of play and “not in play” as a character on the sheet but absent from the current game.
- `[HIGH]` An off-sheet/off-script role is not legal under the ordinary instructions.
- `[HIGH]` Minion, Demon, Traveller, and Fabled characters are not ordinary legal selected “good characters,” even if a particular Traveller is good-aligned.
- The Goblin jinx is the sole verified exception: when it applies, the Cerenovus may select the evil Minion Goblin.

## selfTargetRule

- `[HIGH]` Self-targeting is legal. “Any player” contains no “another player” or “not yourself” restriction, so the Cerenovus may select themself.

## deadTargetRule

- `[HIGH]` A dead player is legal. Both official and Chinese Cerenovus pages provide or discuss dead-target examples.
- A dead target may still be executed for breaking madness. Executing an already-dead player still consumes the day’s single execution, even though it causes no additional death.

## travellerRule

- `[HIGH]` A Traveller player is a legal target because the instruction says any player and the Glossary’s `Player` definition includes anyone with an in-play character.
- `[HIGH]` A Traveller character is not an ordinary legal selected “good character”; Cerenovus operational selection is Townsfolk/Outsider, not alignment-based Traveller selection.
- `[HIGH]` A Traveller cannot receive the execution penalty: official rules define execution as applying to a non-Traveller and treat Traveller removal as exile. Exile is not execution and abilities cannot alter an exile decision.
- Therefore, targeting a Traveller can create the private instruction/madness incentive but cannot culminate in Cerenovus execution.

## drunkRule

- `[HIGH — hard gate resolved]` Wake the drunk source and accept a normal-looking player/character selection. Record the selection as a simulated historical choice.
- The drunk source has no ability, so no real `MadnessRequirement`, effective `MAD` reminder, or execution authority exists.
- `[HIGH]` The selected target must still be privately notified through the normal visible process. Official States/Glossary require the Storyteller to act as if the source were sober and not disclose impairment; official and Chinese Cerenovus running instructions unconditionally include the target wake/notification, while the Chinese marker rule separately says not to place the real marker if the source is impaired.
- The target is not told that the notification is simulated and must not receive impairment/effectiveness metadata.
- If the source later becomes sober, the ineffective triggered selection does not retroactively create an effect.

## poisonRule

- `[HIGH — hard gate resolved]` Identical to drunkenness: wake the poisoned source, accept and record the legal-looking selection, privately notify the selected target through the ordinary token sequence, but create no real madness requirement, marker, or execution authority.
- The target must not learn that the source was poisoned or that the requirement is simulated.
- The repository handoff assertion that an impaired Cerenovus does not notify the target is stale and contradicted by the live source combination.

## madnessDuration

- `[HIGH]` The selection and private notification happen during the Cerenovus wake.
- The enforceable “tomorrow” window begins on the next day, continues through the night following that day, and ends at the dawn after that night.
- It does not authorize punishment on the selection night before the next day.
- It ends earlier if the source loses the Cerenovus ability.
- At the next Cerenovus wake, the physical single marker may be moved for the new choice, but the prior target remains under the old requirement through the coming dawn. Canonical implementation must not confuse physical-token scarcity with effect duration.

## targetNotificationRule

- `[HIGH]` Notification is private. Wake only the selected target and show, in order, the `THIS CHARACTER SELECTED YOU` information token, the Cerenovus token, and the selected character token; then put the target back to sleep.
- The source knows the target and character because it made the choice. The Storyteller knows canonical effectiveness. Other players receive no direct notification.
- The exact same viewer-visible content is delivered for an impaired-source simulation. Target/player/AI projections must not expose whether a real requirement exists, source impairment, simulation reason, Storyteller notes, or future execution eligibility.

## executionDiscretionRule

- `[HIGH]` Execution is discretionary, never mandatory. `Might` means the Storyteller chooses whether the consequence occurs.
- The Storyteller first judges whether the target made a sincere, substantial effort to convince the group. A weak token claim, deliberate attempt to be disbelieved, disclosure or strong hint of madness, or contradictory private statement may justify execution.
- Even after finding insufficient madness, the Storyteller may decline to execute, including to avoid allowing an evil target to exploit the consequence.

## executionTimingRule

- `[HIGH]` The Storyteller may execute during the next day or following night while the requirement is active.
- A Cerenovus execution counts as the single execution allowed for that day, including execution of an already-dead player.
- If it happens during the day before the normal execution, announce it, resolve the execution, end the day, and proceed directly to night; there is no second normal execution.
- If that day’s execution has already occurred, no additional Cerenovus execution is available for the same day/night cycle.
- Travellers cannot be executed and therefore cannot receive this penalty.

## markerPlacementRule

- `[HIGH]` For a sober, healthy effective source, place the `MAD` reminder by the selected player after the choice and before privately notifying the target.
- `[HIGH]` For a drunk or poisoned source, do not place a real/effective marker. Any visual bookkeeping or simulated gesture is not canonical madness state and confers no penalty authority.
- The marker represents an ability effect, not merely that a notification was delivered; notification and real requirement must therefore be separate facts.

## markerRemovalRule

- `[HIGH]` Remove the real marker at the dawn ending the following night, or earlier when the source dies, leaves play, changes away from Cerenovus, or otherwise loses the ability.
- If temporary drunkenness/poisoning disables an already-created effect, official guidance allows the marker to be removed or turned upside down/ignored; the effect may resume if the source becomes healthy again before its original expiry.
- Because the physical game supplies one `MAD` reminder, it may be transferred at the next Cerenovus action even though the prior requirement remains logically active until dawn. Canonical effect identity and duration must not be derived from the marker’s current physical location alone.

## characterChangeInteraction

- `[MEDIUM-HIGH, derived from general state/effect rules]` If the target changes character, the Cerenovus requirement remains attached to that player until its normal end. The required claim remains the character selected in the historical Cerenovus choice; it is not recomputed from the target’s new actual character.
- If the source changes away from Cerenovus or otherwise permanently loses that ability, the persistent requirement ends immediately and the marker is removed.
- If a dead Vigormortis-retained Cerenovus changes into a non-Minion, the Vigormortis page explicitly says the retained ability is lost; any Cerenovus effect therefore ends.

## sourceDeathInteraction

- `[HIGH]` Ordinary death makes the Cerenovus lose its ability immediately, ends its outstanding madness effect, and removes the marker.
- `[HIGH]` Vigormortis is an explicit exception: a Minion personally killed by an able, living Vigormortis keeps its ability, and the official page specifically names Cerenovus as continuing to act each night. In that case death alone does not end the Cerenovus ability/effect.
- If the Vigormortis later dies or loses its ability, or the dead Cerenovus stops being a Minion, the retained ability ends.
- Vigormortis retention is outside the bounded slice.

## sourceLeavesPlayInteraction

- `[HIGH]` If the Cerenovus leaves play, changes character, or permanently loses the ability, its ongoing requirement ends immediately and the marker is removed.
- Temporary source impairment is different: an existing persistent effect is inactive while the source is drunk/poisoned and may resume on recovery within the original duration. A choice first made while impaired never retroactively becomes effective.

## storytellerDiscretion

- `[HIGH]` The Storyteller has final judgment over whether the player is sincerely trying to convince the group and whether to impose the optional execution.
- Evidence may include public statements, private conversations the Storyteller hears, credible reports from another player, hints, denials, tone, conduct, and contradictions. Private evidence remains private and must not leak to unauthorized players.
- Players are never compelled to speak or lie; madness supplies an incentive and optional consequence.
- The Storyteller should account for player experience and game balance, but cannot create execution authority from an impaired source.
- Legal target and character choices belong to the Cerenovus, not the Storyteller.

## jinxes

- `[HIGH]` One verified Cerenovus jinx exists: `Cerenovus ↔ Goblin`.
- Rule: the Cerenovus may make a player mad that they are the Goblin.
- Both official Cerenovus and official Goblin pages contain the reciprocal jinx entry; the Chinese Cerenovus page agrees.
- This is a narrow exception to the normal Townsfolk/Outsider selected-character rule. It applies only when the script/jinx context includes both roles.
- No other Cerenovus jinx appears on the current official Cerenovus page.
