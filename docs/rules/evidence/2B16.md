## abilityRules

- Each night, Cerenovus chooses one player and one good character; operational rules narrow the ordinary character to a Townsfolk or Outsider icon on the current sheet.
- Any player is a legal target, including self, evil players, dead players, and Traveller players. Current code may validate roster membership only and must not claim life-state completeness.
- The selected Townsfolk or Outsider may be in or out of play; ordinary off-script, Minion, Demon, Traveller, and Fabled characters are not legal selections.
- A healthy effective choice establishes a real `MAD` marker/effect and privately informs only the target that Cerenovus selected them and which character to claim. The target does not learn which player is Cerenovus.
- The instruction covers the next day and following night. The real effect normally ends at the following dawn or earlier when the source loses the Cerenovus ability.
- Execution is optional and depends on Storyteller judgment. A Cerenovus execution consumes that day’s single execution, including when the target is already dead; Travellers cannot be executed.

## interactions

- Goblin: a verified jinx permits Goblin as a selected character when the applicable script/jinx context contains both roles. This exception is not implemented.
- Vigormortis: a Minion personally killed by an able living Vigormortis may retain its ability; ordinary death otherwise removes the Cerenovus ability. This lifecycle is not implemented.
- Philosopher: the current duplicate-role drunkenness producer cannot canonically affect an active base Cerenovus because the choice is constrained to a good character.
- Snake Charmer: current Demon-hit poisoning changes the affected old Demon into Snake Charmer first and cannot leave an active base Cerenovus poisoned.
- Vortox: no direct Cerenovus information transformation applies; see `VortoxRules`.
- Target character/alignment changes do not rewrite the historical selected character. Source character/ability loss ends the effect; source alignment change alone does not change the character or ability.
- No generic impairment, lifecycle, jinx, Vortox, character-change, or alignment-change runtime is authorized.

## drunkennessRules

- A drunk Cerenovus has no ability but should be treated as if acting normally: wake the source, accept a normal-looking legal selection, and privately perform the ordinary target notification without disclosing impairment.
- Do not establish a real marker, enforceable madness requirement, or execution authority. Recovery does not retroactively make the ineffective choice real.
- Slice 2B16 does not implement this simulation.
- Current canonical history cannot create Philosopher-duplicate drunkenness on a still-active base Cerenovus.
- A constructed impairment may test only the pure capability gate and must not be presented as canonical history.
- Effective-only settlement fails generically before metadata, events, receipt creation, opportunity closure, or version change.

## poisoningRules

- A poisoned Cerenovus follows the same visible simulation as a drunk Cerenovus: normal-looking legal choice and ordinary private notification, with no real marker, requirement, or execution authority.
- Recovery does not retroactively activate the choice. Slice 2B16 does not implement this simulation.
- The current Snake Charmer producer changes the old Demon into Snake Charmer before poison and cannot leave an active base Cerenovus poisoned.
- Current unreachability is not immunity. Constructed poison state is allowed only for the pure fail-closed capability-gate test.

## VortoxRules

- Official Vortox revision `3017` says Townsfolk abilities yield false information while Vortox is active. Official and Chinese sources distinguish Townsfolk-ability information from rules explanations and character/alignment-change notification.
- Cerenovus is a Minion, not a Townsfolk. Its selection, marker establishment, and target instruction are not a Townsfolk information-yield ability.
- No retrieved source makes Vortox replace the selected character, suppress/remove the marker, falsify the Cerenovus identity shown to the target, or substitute another instruction.
- The target instruction communicates the Cerenovus effect and must not arbitrarily be made false because Vortox is present.
- Vortox may indirectly affect information a mad player invents while bluffing as a Townsfolk; that is player behavior and Storyteller judgment, not transformation of this event chain.
- Slice 2B16 adds no Vortox runtime behavior and interaction coverage remains incomplete.

## characterChangeRules

- Character and alignment are independent states.
- If the source changes away from Cerenovus or permanently loses the ability, the ongoing effect ends and its marker should be removed. A newly acquired Cerenovus instance does not resume an earlier instance’s ended requirement.
- If the target changes character, the requirement remains attached to that player for its original duration and the required claim remains the historically selected character.
- Character-change execution, effect removal, gained Cerenovus ability, and lifecycle processing are outside Slice 2B16.

## alignmentChangeRules

- Official States and Glossary establish that alignment and character are independent. Changing alignment does not change character; changing character does not inherently change alignment.
- Ordinary selected-character legality is based on character type, `TOWNSFOLK | OUTSIDER`, not the current alignment of a player holding the character.
- An evil Townsfolk/Outsider remains an ordinary legal selected character. A good Minion/Demon does not become an ordinary legal selected character.
- Target alignment does not alter target legality, marker establishment, stored selected character, instruction window, or Storyteller madness standard.
- Source alignment change alone does not remove the Cerenovus character or ability; a separate character or ability-loss event is required.
- Slice 2B16 adds no alignment-change command, event, replay flow, projection, or lifecycle handling.

## storytellerDiscretion

- The Storyteller judges whether the target sincerely tried to convince the group. Players are never compelled to speak or lie.
- Public claims, private statements heard by the Storyteller, contradictions, hints, denials, conduct, tone, and player experience may inform judgment.
- Even after finding insufficient effort, execution remains optional; the Storyteller may decline, including to avoid an evil player exploiting the penalty.
- Storyteller discretion cannot create a real marker or execution authority from a drunk or poisoned source.
- Slice 2B16 records only marker policy and instruction history; it does not automate judgment, execution, execution accounting, day ending, or death.
