## abilityRules

1. Philosopher may once per game, at night, choose a good character and gain that character’s ability.
2. Gaining a character’s ability does not change the Philosopher into that character. The resolving player remains the Philosopher; `dreamer` is the gained ability role.
3. If the chosen character is in play, the player who is that character is drunk because of the Philosopher ability.
4. If the gained ability acts at night, the Philosopher acts when that character normally acts.
5. The official Philosopher example specifically states that a Philosopher who chooses Dreamer gains the Dreamer ability and acts when the Dreamer normally acts.
6. Dreamer chooses a player other than the resolving source and other than a Traveller.
7. Dreamer learns exactly one GOOD character and one EVIL character.
8. Without an applicable false-information effect and with an effective source, exactly one of those characters is the target’s true character.
9. GOOD and EVIL describe the characters’ native types:
   - Townsfolk or Outsider is a GOOD character;
   - Minion or Demon is an EVIL character.
10. The pair describes characters, not the target player’s current alignment.
11. Abilities work immediately. The target truth relevant to this resolution is the target’s current true character when the gained Dreamer ability resolves.
12. Information already delivered is historical and is not retroactively rewritten by a later character, alignment, impairment, or Vortox change.

## interactions

### Philosopher source and gained Dreamer ability

- `sourcePlayer` is the player who is the Philosopher.
- `sourceCharacter` remains `philosopher`.
- `gainedAbilityRole` is `dreamer`.
- No Dreamer role-tenure or character-change claim follows merely from gaining the Dreamer ability.
- The Chinese Philosopher page’s physical-token handling is a Storyteller bookkeeping procedure and is explicitly paired with the rule that the Philosopher does not become the chosen character. It does not create a semantic conflict.

### Normal gained Dreamer information

For an effective Philosopher-gained Dreamer source without effective Vortox:

- choose one other non-Traveller player;
- return exactly one GOOD character and one EVIL character;
- exactly one returned character equals the target’s current true character at resolution;
- the other character is a legal character of the opposite native type.

Both GOOD-target and EVIL-target cases are required:

- GOOD target: the true GOOD character plus one legal EVIL character;
- EVIL target: one legal GOOD character plus the true EVIL character.

### Effective Vortox

While a living and effective Vortox applies:

- information yielded by Townsfolk abilities must be false;
- the output shape remains one GOOD plus one EVIL character;
- neither returned character may equal the target’s true character;
- this applies whether the selected target’s true character is GOOD or EVIL.

The official and Chinese Dreamer/Vortox examples confirm that, for a Vortox target, Dreamer may receive a GOOD/EVIL pair containing neither the target’s true character.

### Native Dreamer drunkenness versus gained source

If Philosopher chooses Dreamer while a native/base Dreamer is in play:

- the native/base Dreamer player is the player made drunk by the duplicate choice;
- the Philosopher gains the Dreamer ability;
- the native Dreamer’s DRUNK state does not transfer to the Philosopher source;
- a native Dreamer impairment and a Philosopher source impairment are different player-state facts;
- the gained source is ineffective only if the Philosopher player has an independent applicable DRUNK or POISONED state, dies, or otherwise loses the Philosopher ability.

Therefore, an accepted canonical DRUNK on the native/base Dreamer does not justify treating the Philosopher-gained Dreamer source as impaired.

## drunkennessRules

1. A drunk player has no ability.
2. The Storyteller normally preserves the apparent wake/choice/information procedure so the player need not learn that they are drunk.
3. If the Philosopher becomes drunk after gaining an ability, the Philosopher loses that ability while drunk.
4. The persistent drunkenness caused by that Philosopher ability ends while the Philosopher has no ability; the original duplicated character becomes sober.
5. The native/base Dreamer made drunk by the Philosopher and the Philosopher source are distinct players and distinct impairment subjects.
6. `2B19B` supports only an effective Philosopher source. Successful source-Philosopher DRUNK resolution is outside scope.
7. A represented DRUNK Philosopher path must not be misreported as a successful effective-source gained Dreamer resolution.
8. External rules permit an apparent wake and apparent information procedure for a drunk player, but this Slice does not implement or claim that impaired-source success behavior.

## poisoningRules

1. A poisoned player has no ability; poisoning and drunkenness have the same mechanical no-ability effect.
2. If the Philosopher is poisoned after gaining an ability, that gained ability and its persistent effects do not operate while the Philosopher is poisoned.
3. The duplicated native character becomes sober when the Philosopher’s persistent duplicate effect is inactive.
4. A poisoned Philosopher may still be put through an apparent wake/choice procedure, but it is not an effective gained ability.
5. `2B19B` does not support successful source-Philosopher POISONED information.
6. No Dashii or any other poison producer is outside this Slice.
7. A source-POISONED path must fail closed as unsupported rather than produce an effective-source terminal fact.

## VortoxRules

1. Vortox affects information yielded by Townsfolk abilities.
2. Philosopher remains a Townsfolk character while using the gained Dreamer ability; gaining Dreamer does not change the source character.
3. When Vortox is alive and effective at gained Dreamer resolution, the information must be false.
4. The one-GOOD/one-EVIL Dreamer output shape is preserved.
5. False Dreamer information means neither returned character equals the target’s true character.
6. This constraint also applies to drunk or poisoned Townsfolk generally, although `2B19B` excludes successful source-impairment paths.
7. A dead, drunk, poisoned, stale, duplicated, conflicting, or otherwise unprovable Vortox does not satisfy this Slice’s effective-Vortox success precondition.
8. Vortox does not make character-change or alignment-change notifications false because those facts are not information yielded by a Townsfolk ability.
9. Vortox’s absence from the first-night wake list does not disable its continuous false-information effect.

## characterChangeRules

1. Philosopher gaining Dreamer does not change the Philosopher’s character.
2. The source remains the Philosopher and must not acquire a fabricated Dreamer character tenure.
3. Dreamer information concerns the target’s current true character when the ability resolves.
4. If the target changes character before resolution, the new current character is the truth used for the pair.
5. A character change after delivery does not rewrite the historical information already delivered.
6. General character-change producers and lifecycle behavior are outside this Slice.

## alignmentChangeRules

1. Character and alignment are independent states.
2. Dreamer identifies characters, not player alignments.
3. A target alignment change without a character change does not change the correct Dreamer character.
4. GOOD/EVIL pair slots are determined by the characters’ native types, not by the target player’s present alignment.
5. A later alignment change does not rewrite previously delivered Dreamer information.
6. Philosopher gaining Dreamer does not itself change alignment.

## storytellerDiscretion

1. On the normal effective-source path, the Storyteller may choose any legal false character of the opposite native type while including the target’s true character.
2. Under effective Vortox, the Storyteller must choose a legal GOOD character and a legal EVIL character while excluding the target’s true character from both.
3. A deterministic simulator candidate policy may select one member from the legal set, but that policy is not an official BOTC rule.
4. `2B19B` does not claim complete Storyteller free-choice support.
5. Candidate shortage cannot authorize an illegal pair; it must remain unsupported/fail closed.
