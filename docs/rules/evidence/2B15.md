abilityRules:

- `2B15-R01`: `CHOOSE_TWO_PLAYERS` represents a legal use only when the Seamstress chooses exactly two distinct player entities, neither of whom is the Seamstress.
- `2B15-R02`: Both living and dead players are legal targets. Travellers are legal targets.
- `2B15-R03`: Self-selection, selecting only one player, selecting more than two, selecting the same player twice, or naming a non-player is invalid. “Two players” and “two other players” require two distinct legal players.
- `2B15-R04`: An invalid input is corrected and retried; it is not a completed ability use and does not consume the once-per-game ability.
- `2B15-R05`: A valid two-player choice consumes the once-per-game ability and removes future ordinary Seamstress opportunities.
- `2B15-R06`: Legal use consumes the ability even when the Seamstress is drunk or poisoned. Impairment changes whether the ability works and what may be delivered, not whether the valid once-per-game attempt is spent.
- `2B15-R07`: Abilities work immediately. Determine the alignment comparison at settlement from each target’s current alignment, not their character’s default token colour.
- `2B15-R08`: If the two effective alignments are equal—good/good or evil/evil—the rule-correct answer is `YES`. If one is good and the other evil, it is `NO`.
- `2B15-R09`: A target that registers as another alignment counts as that registered alignment for the Seamstress ability at that resolution.
- `2B15-R10`: The answer is information learned privately by the Seamstress. Other players do not automatically learn the targets or answer.
- `2B15-R11`: A valid use must distinguish the rule-correct comparison from the answer actually delivered. Impairment may permit either answer; Vortox may mandate the false answer; Barista may mandate the true answer.
- `2B15-R12`: Evidence-backed inference: because the ability settles immediately and tells the player at that time, later character, alignment, life, or registration changes do not rewrite, retract, or recompute the already delivered historical information.
- `2B15-R13`: The accepted 2B14 `DEFER` path remains a non-use: no targets, no answer, no consumption, and another future opportunity remains.

interactions:

- `Registration`: Recluse may register as evil and Spy may register as good, even while dead. Their chosen registration counts for the Seamstress comparison.
- `Impaired source`: A drunk or poisoned Seamstress has no working ability but is simulated as acting. A legal choice is still spent, and the Storyteller may deliver either truth value unless another rule constrains it.
- `Impaired target`: A target being drunk or poisoned does not itself make the Seamstress malfunction. However, an impaired Spy or Recluse has lost their own registration ability, so cannot misregister through that ability.
- `Vortox`: An ability-effective Vortox forces Seamstress information false, including when the Seamstress is drunk or poisoned.
- `Philosopher`: A Philosopher gaining the Seamstress ability remains the Philosopher, receives an independent Seamstress ability, and acts at the Seamstress timing. An in-play original Seamstress is drunk while the Philosopher effect remains active.
- `Character change`: A target’s character change normally preserves alignment. A player newly becoming Seamstress receives the new character ability immediately; changing away from Seamstress loses it immediately.
- `Alignment change`: The comparison uses the targets’ alignments at immediate settlement.
- `Death`: Dead players are legal targets, but a dead Seamstress ordinarily has no ability and cannot act.
- `Revival`: A revived player regains their ability, including a once-per-game ability previously used.
- `Barista`: `SOBER AND HEALTHY` forces true information despite Vortox or false registration; `ACTS TWICE` can grant extra once-per-game uses according to Barista’s text.
- `Travellers`: Their current Storyteller-assigned alignment participates normally in same/different comparison.
- `Historical delivery`: Subsequent state changes do not mutate the fact that a particular answer was delivered at settlement.

drunkennessRules:

- A drunk Seamstress has no functioning ability but does not learn that they are drunk.
- They are still simulated as waking, selecting legal targets, and receiving a `YES` or `NO`.
- The Storyteller may give the rule-correct answer or its opposite; false information is permitted, not mandatory.
- A legal two-player use is consumed even while drunk. Becoming sober later does not return it.
- A drunk target remains a valid target and still has an alignment.
- If the target’s own ability enabled false registration, drunkenness disables that registration ability.
- A later onset of drunkenness cannot retroactively alter an answer already delivered.

poisoningRules:

- Poisoning has the same ability-nullification and simulation behavior as drunkenness.
- A poisoned Seamstress may receive either the true or false binary answer.
- A legal two-player attempt remains consumed after the player becomes healthy.
- A poisoned target remains valid and is compared normally.
- A poisoned Spy or Recluse cannot use their own ability to misregister.
- Effective Vortox still mandates false information even when the Seamstress is poisoned.

VortoxRules:

- While Vortox is alive and ability-effective, Townsfolk ability information must be false.
- Seamstress is a Townsfolk character; the player’s own alignment does not remove the Vortox interaction.
- If the rule-correct result is `YES`, the delivered result must be `NO`; if rule-correct is `NO`, delivery must be `YES`.
- The false mandate still applies when the Seamstress is drunk or poisoned.
- If Vortox is dead, drunk, or poisoned, its ability does not independently force false information.
- Where target registration is active, calculate the ability-facing comparison using the selected registrations, then satisfy the Vortox false-information requirement.
- Barista’s explicit true-information exception overrides Vortox.

characterChangeRules:

- A target changing character does not by itself change alignment, so it does not by itself change the Seamstress comparison.
- Official Seamstress material explicitly illustrates a good Mathematician becoming a good Witch and still comparing as good.
- A player becoming Seamstress gains its ability immediately, including a fresh once-per-game use.
- A Seamstress becoming another character loses the Seamstress ability immediately.
- Information already delivered before a character change remains a historical delivery and is not recalculated.
- Philosopher gaining the ability is not a character change; the Philosopher remains the Philosopher.

alignmentChangeRules:

- Alignment is the team a player is currently on.
- A target alignment change before settlement affects the current comparison.
- A change after settlement does not change the already delivered answer.
- A Seamstress changing alignment retains the Seamstress character and its existing spent/unspent state.
- An evil-aligned Seamstress is still a Townsfolk character for Vortox purposes.
- Learning one’s own changed character or alignment is separate information and is not distorted by drunkenness, poisoning, or Vortox.

storytellerDiscretion:

- The Seamstress player chooses both targets; the Storyteller does not select them.
- Invalid input must be rejected and corrected rather than converted into a legal use.
- For a sober, healthy Seamstress with no modifying effects, the Storyteller has no discretion to invert the result.
- With an impaired Seamstress, the Storyteller may deliver either the true or false binary result.
- With active Vortox, the Storyteller must deliver false information, including to an impaired Seamstress.
- With Barista true-information protection, the Storyteller must deliver true information even through Vortox or Spy/Recluse false registration.
- Spy/Recluse registration is a separate Storyteller choice where their abilities are active.
- Only the Seamstress learns the private result; there is no automatic public disclosure.
- Official Storyteller Advice prohibits breaking rules for dramatic effect. Adjudication discretion cannot override deterministic or mandatory truth/falsehood rules.
