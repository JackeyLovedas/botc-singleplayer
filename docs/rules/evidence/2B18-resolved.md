## External rules versus simulator policies

### External rule claims

- Each night, the Mathematician learns how many players’ abilities worked abnormally since dawn due to another character’s ability.
- The count is by affected player, not by number of abnormal incidents. Multiple abnormal outcomes for one player contribute at most one.
- The Mathematician does not detect its own ability failing.
- Drunkenness or poisoning itself does not count. An impaired ability counts only if its actual result differs from how it should have worked.
- Correct information or an otherwise normal result while impaired does not count.
- A Vortox forces Townsfolk ability information to be false, including when the recipient is drunk or poisoned.
- Philosopher-gained nightly abilities act when the gained character would normally act.
- A pending Witch curse is not yet a failed death result. The relevant abnormality exists only if the later trigger occurs and the expected death fails because of another ability.
- Character or alignment changes are not themselves false Townsfolk information.

### Approved simulator policies

These are product contracts, not claims of official BOTC wording:

- `FirstNightInitialized` supplies the fixed first-night lower boundary.
- Self-exclusion is resolved through source-player plus ability-instance/provenance identity, never by excluding all facts whose role ID is `mathematician`.
- The fixed 12-player/no-Traveller numerical domain is the dense integer set `0..11`.
- The smallest-false-value choice is deterministic product policy, not official Storyteller procedure.
- Duplicate Mathematician holders resolve in accepted V2 scheduling order and inspect only completed earlier outcomes.
- `NORMAL`, `ABNORMAL`, `UNRESOLVED`, and `PENDING_TRIGGER` are ledger classifications. `UNRESOLVED` and `PENDING_TRIGGER` are conservative product states, not official reminder-token names.

## Ability rules

- Count distinct qualifying players, never incidents.
- A qualifying counted result must be:
  - within the approved first-night window;
  - terminal for the modeled ability outcome;
  - `ABNORMAL`;
  - caused by another character’s ability;
  - not excluded by the resolving Mathematician identity/temporal contract.
- `NORMAL` contributes zero.
- `PENDING_TRIGGER` contributes zero and does not block a first-night trusted count.
- An eligible `UNRESOLVED` fact that may change the distinct-player result blocks a trusted integer result. Resolution status must remain `UNRESOLVED`.
- Empty ledgers and ledgers containing only `NORMAL` or non-triggered `PENDING_TRIGGER` facts resolve to true count `0`.
- A later duplicate holder may count an earlier holder only after the earlier holder has completed and only when that earlier ability actually worked abnormally due to another character’s ability.
- Earlier results are immutable after delivery; later events never retroactively alter them.

## Drunkenness rules

- A drunk player has no functioning ability but is normally simulated as though acting.
- Drunkenness itself is not an abnormal ability outcome.
- Correct information or a result identical to normal while drunk is `NORMAL`.
- A proven deviation caused by drunkenness is `ABNORMAL`.
- A represented impairment without sufficient historical result evidence is not automatically `ABNORMAL`; use `UNRESOLVED` only where the missing evidence may change the count.
- Philosopher’s duplicate `DRUNK` marker is causal evidence, not by itself a counted outcome.

## Poisoning rules

- Poisoning has the same loss-of-ability effect as drunkenness.
- Poisoning itself does not count.
- Correct information or a result identical to normal while poisoned is `NORMAL`.
- A proven deviation caused by poisoning is `ABNORMAL`.
- Snake Charmer’s poisoned non-Demon no-swap remains `NORMAL`; poisoned Demon no-swap is `ABNORMAL`.
- Unsupported continuous poison derivation must not be inferred from current state or model memory.

## Vortox rules

- Effective Vortox requires Townsfolk ability information to be false.
- This remains true if the information recipient is drunk or poisoned.
- Every distinct affected player whose information is forced abnormal can contribute once.
- The current Mathematician’s own delivery never feeds its own current true count.
- Under the approved duplicate temporal policy, a later holder may inspect an earlier completed Mathematician delivery.
- Product numerical candidates are `0..11`.
- Effective non-Vortox information must equal `trueCount`.
- Ordinary drunk/poisoned non-Vortox information may use any `0..11` value, including `trueCount`; the deterministic product policy selects the smallest false value.
- Effective Vortox permits only `0..11` values unequal to `trueCount` and selects the smallest false value.
- “Smallest false” is simulator policy, not an official rule.
- 2B18A does not perform this final candidate selection or delivery.

## Character-change rules

- Completed outcome facts bind the historical character-state revision and are never recomputed from a later character.
- Snake Charmer’s successful Demon swap is `NORMAL`; the before/after historical states establish that result.
- Ability-instance/provenance identity, not role ID alone, distinguishes base and Philosopher-gained abilities.
- A later character change cannot alter an earlier ledger classification or earlier Mathematician result.
- Unsupported Pit-Hag, Barber, general character creation, and general role-lifecycle paths remain outside scope.

## Alignment-change rules

- Snake Charmer’s normal alignment exchange on a Demon hit is part of the successful normal outcome.
- Seamstress uses the alignment relationship stored/evaluated at its historical settlement, not later alignment.
- Character/alignment-change notifications are not Townsfolk ability information for Vortox purposes.

## Storyteller discretion

- Effective non-Vortox information must be true.
- Drunk or poisoned information may be true or false.
- Vortox-constrained Townsfolk information must be false.
- The approved `0..11` domain and smallest-false deterministic choice constrain this simulator only.
- No unrestricted Storyteller choice, arbitrary hand signal, Traveller-expanded domain, or variable-player-count policy is implemented here.
- Ledger classification must follow proven historical facts; discretion must not be reconstructed from newer state.

## Ledger/count interpretation required by rule evidence

- `NORMAL`: enough historical evidence proves the ability worked according to its normal rule.
- `ABNORMAL`: enough historical evidence proves another character’s ability caused a deviation.
- `UNRESOLVED`: accepted history lacks enough evidence and the missing fact may change the count.
- `PENDING_TRIGGER`: the rule-defined trigger has not reached its final adjudication point.
- Neither `UNRESOLVED` nor `PENDING_TRIGGER` may be silently converted to `NORMAL`.
- `PENDING_TRIGGER` does not block the present first-night true count.
- A count-relevant `UNRESOLVED` state prevents a trusted integer.
- Count distinct qualifying `sourcePlayerId` values once.
- Self/current-resolution exclusion uses source plus ability-instance/provenance identity and must not globally remove another holder’s Mathematician fact by `roleId`.
- Strict temporal bounds prohibit circular or retroactive counting.
