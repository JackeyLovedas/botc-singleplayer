## abilityRules

1. Dreamer chooses another player. The chosen player cannot be the Dreamer and cannot
   be a Traveller.
2. A functioning Dreamer is shown one good character and one evil character, one of
   which is the chosen player's actual character.
3. If the Philosopher gains the Dreamer ability while a Dreamer is already in play,
   the already-in-play base Dreamer becomes drunk because of the Philosopher's
   ability.
4. The accepted 2B20A stream has the original base Dreamer as the planned
   `DREAMER_ACTION` source. The Philosopher's gained Dreamer task is not a substitute
   for that independently planned base task.
5. The exact 2B20A settlement must still use the Dreamer's apparent action: a legal
   target is chosen, an apparent one-good/one-evil pair is delivered, and the real
   base task is formally settled.
6. The information pair is a delivered private fact. Canonical impairment provenance,
   actual character truth, and Storyteller candidate reasoning are not player
   knowledge.

## interactions

### Philosopher and base Dreamer

- The Philosopher's accepted choice of Dreamer is the cause of the base Dreamer's
  drunkenness.
- The canonical source player is the Philosopher, not the drunk Dreamer and not the
  selected target.
- The base Dreamer remains the action holder and receives the private apparent pair.
- The base task must be settled through the existing Dreamer opportunity, target,
  delivery, and settlement framework; a synthetic skip is not legal.

### Dreamer and Mathematician

- The existence of a `DRUNK` marker is not itself a Mathematician count.
- If the apparent pair is rule-correct for the target, the Dreamer ability worked
  correctly for this evaluation and adds `0`.
- If the apparent pair is false because the Philosopher's ability made the base
  Dreamer drunk, the Philosopher source player is counted once.
- If no Dreamer delivery occurred, there is no Dreamer outcome to count.
- One outcome cannot count both the Philosopher and Dreamer, and cannot count the
  Philosopher twice.

## drunkennessRules

1. A drunk character has no ability, but the Storyteller pretends that the ability is
   functioning.
2. The drunk player is not told that they are drunk.
3. For an information ability, the Storyteller may provide information as though the
   ability worked; the information may be true or false within the apparent ability's
   structural constraints.
4. For this Dreamer interaction, the apparent delivery must still contain exactly one
   good role and one evil role.
5. A true pair contains the target's actual character. A false pair does not contain
   the target's actual character.
6. A false pair is not obtained by mechanically negating a previously computed true
   pair. It is a separate legal Storyteller choice from valid candidates.
7. The player/AI projection must not expose drunkenness, its Philosopher provenance,
   the truth classification, or canonical candidate sets.

## poisoningRules

- Poisoning and drunkenness both make a character's ability not work, but their
  provenance is distinct.
- 2B20A does not add a poisoned-Dreamer success path.
- An accepted `POISONED` Dreamer, conflicting impairment provenance, or a noncanonical
  impairment must remain unsupported and fail closed.
- No Dashii poisoning derivation remains a separate future slice.

## VortoxRules

- A healthy, effective current Vortox forces Townsfolk information to be false.
- 2B20A has no current Vortox: the current Demon is Fang Gu.
- The apparent pair may therefore be true or false because the Dreamer is drunk; it
  is not forced false by Vortox.
- Existing effective-current-Vortox and canonical-drunk/effective-Vortox paths are
  regression authority and must not be reinterpreted.
- Impaired, dead, poisoned, drunk, or otherwise ineffective Vortox behavior is outside
  2B20A.

## alignmentChangeRules

## storytellerDiscretion

- The Storyteller chooses the apparent pair from legal one-good/one-evil candidates.
- For the drunk non-Vortox Dreamer, the Storyteller may choose a true pair containing
  the target's actual character or a false pair excluding it.
- Candidate legality is rule-governed; final selection among legal candidates is
  Storyteller discretion.
- A deterministic candidate ordering or deterministic final-choice procedure is a
  simulator policy for reproducibility, not an official BOTC rule.
- Any future deterministic policy must record legal candidates and the final choice,
  remain stable across environments, and not use locale or uncontrolled randomness.
