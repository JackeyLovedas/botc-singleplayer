## abilityRules

1. Mathematician learns the number of players whose abilities worked abnormally since dawn because of another character‚Äôs ability.
2. The count unit is a player, not a fact, output field, cause, ability instance, or malfunction incident.
3. The Chinese Mathematician detailed procedure explicitly says the same player receives at most one abnormal reminder even if their ability malfunctions multiple times that day or they change character and malfunction under multiple characters.
4. Consequently, multiple qualifying terminal facts belonging to one source player contribute exactly one player. Those facts may all remain supporting evidence, but they do not increase `trueCount`.
5. Qualifying facts belonging to different source players contribute separately even when both facts concern the same gained/native ability role.
6. Philosopher gains Dreamer‚Äôs ability but does not become Dreamer. A native Dreamer player and a different Philosopher player remain two distinct players and therefore are two possible Mathematician contributors.
7. Only an actual abnormal result caused by another character‚Äôs ability qualifies. The presence of DRUNK or POISONED alone does not qualify.
8. A drunk or poisoned ability that nevertheless produces its normal result does not qualify.
9. `NORMAL` and non-terminal/pending facts do not contribute.
10. The Mathematician does not detect its own ability failing.
11. Under `BOTC-SIM-MATHEMATICIAN-OWN-ABILITY-EXCLUSION-V1`, the current resolving player and current resolving Mathematician ability instance are excluded; this is not a blanket exclusion of all facts whose ability role is Mathematician.
12. `trueCount` must be derived from all distinct qualifying source players within the actual frozen window. It must never be hard-coded as `2`.
13. In the exact authorized scenario, if the only qualifying source players are native Dreamer `P1` and Philosopher `P2`, the dynamic result is:

```text
distinctAbnormalPlayers = [P1, P2]
trueCount = 2
```

## interactions

### Native Dreamer fact

1. A real effective Philosopher choosing Dreamer makes the in-play native Dreamer player DRUNK.
2. Effective Vortox requires the apparent Dreamer information to be false even though the native Dreamer is drunk.
3. Under `BOTC-SIM-DREAMER-VORTOX-DRUNK-LEDGER-ATTRIBUTION-V1`, the bounded simulator records exactly one terminal fact:

```text
sourcePlayerId = native Dreamer P1
outcomeStatus = ABNORMAL
causeKind = VORTOX_FALSE_INFORMATION
causedByAnotherCharacterAbility = true
exact Philosopher-produced DRUNK evidence = required
second terminal fact = false
```

4. This makes `P1` one qualifying player, not one count per returned role, impairment fact, cause, or evidence record.

### Philosopher-gained Dreamer fact

1. The Philosopher remains the source player and character while holding the gained Dreamer ability.
2. The native Dreamer‚Äôs DRUNK status does not transfer to the Philosopher.
3. For an effective Philosopher source under an effective Vortox, the gained Dreamer information must be false.
4. The resulting terminal fact is attributable to the Philosopher source:

```text
sourcePlayerId = Philosopher P2
outcomeStatus = ABNORMAL
causeKind = VORTOX_FALSE_INFORMATION
causedByAnotherCharacterAbility = true
```

5. Because `P2 != P1`, this is a second qualifying player even though both terminal facts involve Dreamer ability semantics.

### Combined Mathematician result

If both facts validate and fall inside the current window:

```text
qualifyingPlayers = {P1, P2}
trueCount = cardinality(qualifyingPlayers) = 2
```

A second qualifying fact for either `P1` or `P2` expands that player‚Äôs supporting evidence but does not make the total `3`.

### Mathematician‚Äôs own Vortox delivery

1. The true distinct-player count remains `2`.
2. An effective Vortox also requires the effective Townsfolk Mathematician‚Äôs delivered number to be false.
3. The Vortox constraint changes `selectedCount`; it does not change `trueCount` or reclassify the underlying qualifying facts.
4. Official and Chinese examples both use a true abnormal count of `6` while delivering different false values (`4` and `3` respectively), confirming that the false value is not uniquely prescribed.
5. Under `BOTC-SIM-MATHEMATICIAN-NUMERIC-DOMAIN-V1`, the simulator uses `0..11` and its accepted smallest-false policy. Therefore, for an effective Mathematician and effective Vortox in the frozen `trueCount=2` scenario:

```text
trueCount = 2
selectedCount = 0
informationReliability = VORTOX_FALSE
```

This `selectedCount=0` is the existing product policy, not an official uniquely required false number.

## drunkennessRules

1. A drunk player has no ability, but the Storyteller normally preserves the apparent wake, choice, and information procedure.
2. Apparent information may be correct or incorrect while drunk; incorrect information is permitted, not mandatory, absent another constraint.
3. An effective Vortox narrows that discretion: apparent Townsfolk information must be false even when the source is drunk.
4. Drunkenness itself is not a Mathematician contribution. Only the resulting abnormal ability behavior qualifies.
5. The exact native Dreamer DRUNK is produced by the Philosopher and must exist before native Dreamer settlement.
6. If the Philosopher dies or becomes drunk or poisoned, its persistent duplicate-caused DRUNK ends. Such a history does not satisfy this Slice‚Äôs exact native-DRUNK success precondition.
7. The native Dreamer DRUNK does not impair the separate Philosopher source.
8. A DRUNK Philosopher/gained source success path is outside scope.

## poisoningRules

1. Poisoning and drunkenness have the same no-ability mechanical effect.
2. Poisoning alone is not counted by Mathematician.
3. A poisoned ability that produces its normal result is not counted.
4. Without effective Vortox, a poisoned information player may receive true or false information.
5. With effective Vortox, apparent Townsfolk information must be false even while poisoned.
6. This Slice does not authorize a POISONED native or Philosopher-gained Dreamer success path, a No Dashii producer, or a general poisoning engine.
7. A poisoned Philosopher does not have the gained Dreamer ability while poisoned, and its persistent duplicate drunkenness is inactive.

## VortoxRules

1. While Vortox is alive and effective, Townsfolk abilities yield false information.
2. This applies even if the information source is drunk or poisoned.
3. Vortox changes truthfulness, not the Dreamer output shape or the Mathematician‚Äôs underlying true distinct-player total.
4. Dreamer must still receive one native GOOD and one native EVIL character; under Vortox neither may be the target‚Äôs true character.
5. Vortox-caused false information is an ability working abnormally because of another character‚Äôs ability. The official and Chinese Mathematician examples expressly include Vortox-caused false Townsfolk information among the actual abnormalities.
6. Therefore, each of the two validated Dreamer facts qualifies independently when it is inside the first-night window.
7. Vortox also makes Mathematician‚Äôs delivered information false, but Mathematician cannot count its own current failure.
8. Dead, drunk, poisoned, stale, duplicated, conflicting, or otherwise unprovable Vortox applicability is outside the success path.

## characterChangeRules

1. Philosopher gaining Dreamer‚Äôs ability is not a character change; the source remains Philosopher.
2. Mathematician aggregation is by player. The Chinese detailed rule expressly says that the same player remains at most one abnormal marker even when changing character and malfunctioning under different characters that day.
3. A source player therefore cannot evade deduplication merely by changing character or ability instance.
4. Two different players are not deduplicated merely because their facts share `abilityRoleId=dreamer`.
5. Dreamer truth concerns the target‚Äôs current true character when the ability resolves.
6. A later character change cannot retroactively rewrite already delivered information or a historical terminal outcome.
7. General character-change producers, dynamic task recalculation, and source-character-change success paths are outside scope.

## alignmentChangeRules

1. Character and alignment are independent.
2. Philosopher gaining Dreamer does not change the Philosopher‚Äôs alignment.
3. Dreamer GOOD/EVIL slots concern native character type, not the target player‚Äôs current alignment.
4. Alignment changes do not merge or split player identity for Mathematician distinct-player counting.
5. A later alignment change does not rewrite historical Dreamer or Mathematician information.
6. No general alignment-change producer is included.

## storytellerDiscretion

1. The actual Mathematician `trueCount` is determined by the distinct qualifying players; it is not discretionary.
2. Ordinary drunk or poisoned information may be true or false.
3. Effective Vortox requires false information but does not prescribe one unique false number.
4. The official `4` and Chinese `3` Vortox examples for the same true count are both legal and are not a substantive conflict.
5. The simulator‚Äôs `0..11` numeric domain and smallest-false choice are approved deterministic product policies.
6. Dreamer false-role choice remains Storyteller discretion within the legal one-GOOD/one-EVIL shape and applicable truth constraint. This Slice does not expand or complete free Storyteller choice support.
